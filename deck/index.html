The problem
$15–20B
left uncollected every year in the US — money insurers are owed but never recover.

When your insurer pays for a crash that wasn't your fault, it has the right to claw that money back from the at-fault driver's insurer. That's subrogation.

It's buried in paper

Police reports, photos, repair invoices, medical bills, event-data recorders, policies, statutes — one case is a stack of documents.

It's on a clock

Recovery deadlines are tight. Manual review is slow. The math rarely gets done before the window closes.

So cases get dropped

Thousands of viable recoveries are delayed or quietly abandoned — pure lost revenue for the carrier.

A real case

Alex ran a green light. Jordan ran the red.

Jordan T-bones Alex's door. Alex's car is totaled, the medical bills land, and the loss comes to $42,000. Alex's insurer pays fast — but Alex didn't cause this. Jordan's carrier should pay it back.

How it works — a bounded courtroom, not a chatbot

Evidence becomes a hearing.

01
Ingest
Reports, photos, invoices & bills → extracted text.
02
Evidence Ledger
A typed graph of facts — each one quoting its source, verbatim.
03
The Hearing
Counsel argue the docket of legal issues, point by point.
04
Adjudicate
Neutral judges set fault & dollars — with shown math.
05
Packet
Demand letter drafted, reconciled, hashed & escalated.

A Court Clerk locks the ledger and opens a docket — primary liability, comparative fault, damages, legal basis. Recovery Counsel and Defense Counsel brief each issue independently; defense cross-examines, recovery redirects. The debaters never decide the outcome — neutral adjudicators do.

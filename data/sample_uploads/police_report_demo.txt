CALIFORNIA TRAFFIC COLLISION REPORT
Report Number: SAC-2026-09231
Investigating Agency: Sacramento Police Department, Traffic Division
Date of Collision: April 2, 2026          Time: 17:24 hours
Location: Intersection of Broadway and 5th Avenue, Sacramento, CA

PARTIES
Party 1 (Insured / our claimant): Priya Sharma, operating a 2024 Honda Accord,
  license 7KLM402, traveling westbound on Broadway.
Party 2 (Other driver): Marcus Webb, operating a 2020 Chevrolet Silverado,
  license 5RTP918, traveling southbound on 5th Avenue.

NARRATIVE
At approximately 17:24, Party 1 (Sharma) entered the intersection of Broadway and
5th Avenue while facing a steady green signal in the westbound direction. Party 2
(Webb) entered the same intersection traveling southbound and failed to stop for a
steady red signal, striking the driver side of Party 1's vehicle.

Independent witness Thomas Nguyen, waiting at the southeast corner, stated: "The
Honda clearly had the green. The pickup never slowed down — it ran the red light
and T-boned her." The posted speed limit on 5th Avenue at this location is 30 mph.
Data from the Silverado's event data recorder shows a pre-impact speed of 48 mph.
Signal timing records from the City of Sacramento confirm the westbound Broadway
approach held a green indication at the moment of impact.

CITATION
Party 2 (Marcus Webb) was cited at the scene under California Vehicle Code section
21453(a) for failing to stop at a steady red signal. No citation was issued to
Party 1.

VEHICLE DAMAGE
Party 1 (Honda Accord): heavy damage to the driver side doors, B-pillar, and front
wheel; side curtain airbags deployed. Vehicle towed from scene.
Party 2 (Chevrolet Silverado): moderate front-end damage.

INJURIES
Party 1 reported left arm and rib pain and was transported for evaluation. No
injuries reported by Party 2.

OFFICER ASSESSMENT
Based on the physical evidence, the witness statement, the EDR data, the signal
timing records, and the citation issued, the primary cause of this collision was
Party 2's failure to stop for a red signal in violation of CVC 21453(a).
